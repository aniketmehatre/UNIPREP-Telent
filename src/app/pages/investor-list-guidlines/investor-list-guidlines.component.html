INVESTOR LIST GUIDELINES
  1. Research Before Contacting: Thoroughly research potential investors to ensure they align with your business goals and values.
  2. Personalize Communications: Tailor your messages to each investor, demonstrating your understanding of their investment interests and preferences.
  3. Provide Relevant Information: Offer clear and concise information about your venture, highlighting key points that may resonate with the investor.
  4. Be Professional: Maintain a professional tone and demeanor in all communications, showcasing your commitment and credibility as an entrepreneur.
  5. Follow-up appropriately: Follow up with investors in a timely manner, respecting their time and preferences for communication.
  1. Spamming: Avoid sending mass or unsolicited messages to investors, as this can be perceived as unprofessional and may harm your reputation.
  2. Overwhelming with Information: Refrain from overwhelming investors with excessive or irrelevant information about your venture.
  3. Being Pushy: Avoid being overly aggressive or pushy in your communications with investors, as this can be off-putting and may damage your relationship with them.
  4. Ignoring Feedback: Don't dismiss or ignore feedback from investors, as it can provide valuable insights and opportunities for improvement.
  5. Misrepresentation: Avoid misrepresenting your venture or its potential to investors, as this can erode trust and credibility in the long run.
